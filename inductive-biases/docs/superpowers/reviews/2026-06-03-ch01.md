### [M2] Gradient-check numbers in prose use slightly rounded values that could mislead

- **Location:** `book/chapters/ch01/06-stability-to-autograd.tex:91-95`
- **Finding:** The prose reports four gradient-check errors. Two are exact matches to the notebook; two are rounded in ways that overstate precision. Specifically, the prose says "ReLU MLP (SigmoidBCE): $\approx 1 \times 10^{-9}$" but the notebook outputs `9.96e-10`, which rounds naturally to $\approx 1 \times 10^{-9}$, so this is fine. The prose says "Tanh MLP (SoftmaxCE): $\approx 7 \times 10^{-9}$" and the notebook outputs `6.56e-09`, which rounds to $\approx 7 \times 10^{-9}$. These are within normal single-significant-figure rounding. However, the Results cell of the notebook rounds ReLU MLP to $\approx 6 \times 10^{-10}$ while the prose says $\approx 1 \times 10^{-9}$. The actual output is `9.96e-10`, so both approximations are defensible. The inconsistency is between the notebook's own Results cell ($6 \times 10^{-10}$) and the prose ($1 \times 10^{-9}$). The actual value `9.96e-10` is closer to $10^{-9}$ than to $6 \times 10^{-10}$.
- **Auditors:** math-auditor, spec-auditor (notebook-as-truth discipline)
- **Suggested fix:** Update the Results cell of the notebook to say "$\approx 1 \times 10^{-9}$" for the ReLU MLP entry to match what the prose reports, or vice versa. The prose value is more accurate; the notebook Results cell should be corrected to remove the discrepancy.
