## SUBSTANTIVE Findings

### [S1] Chapter 6 M-scaling iteration count is internally inconsistent (3000 vs 6000)
- **Type:** cross-chapter is not the issue; this is a per-chapter (Ch6) provenance inconsistency. Lenses: math-auditor, quality-auditor.
- **Locations:**
  - `book/chapters/ch06/03-scaling-and-investigation.tex:23` (scaling table header: "Transformer (2L, 6000 it)")
  - `book/chapters/ch06/03-scaling-and-investigation.tex:46` (`fig:ch06-scaling` caption: "both models trained for 3000 iterations")
  - `notebooks/ch06-transformer.ipynb` (sweep cell `n_iters=3000`; figure title "Single-lookup accuracy vs memory size (3000 iters)"; Results cell prints "Scaling sweep ... 3000 iters")
  - `examples/RESULTS.md` M=16/24/32 sections (baseline sinusoidal 2L 1H recorded at **6000** iters: 0.747, 0.687, 0.664)
- **Finding:** The same accuracy data points (M=16 0.747, M=24 0.687, M=32 0.664) are attributed to **6000 iterations** in the prose table and in `RESULTS.md`, but to **3000 iterations** in the figure caption and throughout the notebook (which hardcodes those exact values from `RESULTS.md` and labels them "3000 iters"). One of the two iteration counts is wrong for these numbers. `RESULTS.md` is the recorded source of truth and lists them under 6000-iter runs, so the figure caption and the notebook's "3000 iters" labels appear to be the error; alternatively the table header is the stray. Either way a reader who looks at the table, the figure, and the notebook sees three artifacts disagreeing on how long the transformer was trained for the headline plateau result.
- **Why it matters (substantive, not minor):** the plateau-near-chance result is the entire pivot of the chapter (it motivates the four-hypothesis investigation and the third axis). The reader is invited to trust that the transformer genuinely cannot scale; the credibility of that rests on the training budget being reported correctly and consistently. A 2x ambiguity in the budget on the load-bearing experiment is more than a typo.
- **Suggested fix:** Pick the count that matches the recorded `RESULTS.md` runs (6000 for the M>=16 baseline) and make the figure caption and the notebook's sweep `n_iters`, figure title, and Results-cell label agree with it; or, if the intended sweep really was 3000 iters, correct the table header and reconcile `RESULTS.md`. Then re-confirm the figure caption. Note: this is a reconciliation of a stated number, not a re-run requirement, the accuracy values themselves are consistent across all artifacts.

### [S2] Chapter 6 scaling figure caption omits the cited-data provenance that the prose states
- **Type:** per-chapter (Ch6). Lens: quality-auditor, spec-auditor (Decision B / S1 "generate is not place" lineage).
- **Location:** `book/chapters/ch06/03-scaling-and-investigation.tex:41-50` (`fig:ch06-scaling` caption) vs body line 39 ("plots the two curves from the recorded runs in `examples/RESULTS.md`").
- **Finding:** The body text correctly states the scaling curve is plotted from recorded `RESULTS.md` data (the sanctioned approach per the Ch6 plan Section 3, since the live NumPy sweep is ~30 min). The figure caption does not carry that provenance, so the figure reads as a live receipt when it is a plot of cited points. The grokking figure (`fig:ch06-grokking`) is the live re-run; the scaling figure is not, and the caption should not let a reader assume otherwise.
- **Why it matters:** the book's notebook-first discipline (master spec Section 7, Part I lesson) treats "is this number a live receipt or a citation" as a first-class honesty property. The prose is honest; the caption should match.
- **Suggested fix:** add a short clause to the `fig:ch06-scaling` caption, e.g. "Points are the recorded runs from `examples/RESULTS.md`; see text," mirroring the in-text statement. (Pairs naturally with the S1 fix since both touch this caption.)
